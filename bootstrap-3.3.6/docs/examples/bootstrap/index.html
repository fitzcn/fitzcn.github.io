Sorting out sorting

An introduction to computational thinking using sorting algorithms

Overview

The concept of algorithms and their design can be difficult to grasp in the abstract. The purpose of this lesson is to first build out studentsâ€™ understanding of how algorithms are implemented through activities and pseudocode, then apply those ideas in an exploration of commonly known sorting algorithms.

Order of activities

The lesson is broken into four different sections, each with a distinct goal.

  1. Algorithms role and function
  2. Sorting Activities
  3. Pseudocode/Implementation
  4. Ways to evaluate

Student Outcomes

Students will be able to:

  1. Identify problem spaces for algorithmic intervention
  2. Generate pseudocode for algorithm design
  3. Make implementation decisions given the problem space
  4. Evaluate algorithms

Algorithms' Role and Function

We encounter algorithms constantly in our daily routines. In this section of the workshop, we begin our discussion on the roles of algorithms in shaping our experiences.

What do algorithms do for you?

Working with a partner, work on the following questions/prompts.

  • Find an example of when you think you encountered an algorithm yesterday.
  • Describe the context and setting. What were you trying to do?
  • What did this algorithm do for you?
  • What alternatives did you have?
  • What steps did the algorithm take to come up with its response to you?
  • What decisions or assumptions were made in the algorithms' design?

A working definition of algorithm

Given the discussion from the previous task, work independently to write down a working definition of an algorithm. Once done, share your definition with your partner. When the partners are done sharing, we will come together as a class to create a single working definition.

Given this our new working definition, what do we think of the following:

  • What can be considered an algorithm?
  • What is not an algorithm?
  • Where else do we encounter algorithms in our lives?
  • What are examples of non-digital algorithms?
  • What considerations are important in the development of algorithms?
