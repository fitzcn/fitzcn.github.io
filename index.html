Colin Fitzpatrick

PhD student of technology and social behavior

About me

I am delighted to be in my third year of Northwestern University’s PhD program in Technology and Social Behavior. A unique combination of computer science and communication studies, the faculty that I work with live at the intersection of research and design. I am currently developing my skills as both a designer and a developer, focusing on mixed methods approaches to studying social media.

I attended Brown University for my B.A. in ethnomusicology, where I studied the role of technology in impression management of music consumption practices. In 2012 I completed a master’s of the arts in ethnomusicology at UW-Madison, focusing on the role of technology in music production and consumption in 20th and 21st century China. At Northwestern, I am a member of the Social Media Lab, led by Professor Jeremy Birnholtz, where I study location-aware mobile social networking applications.

Research

My research has continuously focused on the relationship between technology and contexts of use, specific to time, place, and audience. In undergrad, I investigated how online profiles shifted a private music consumption to a public audience; in my master's program I examined how recording technology collapsed time, distance, and audiences in ballrooms and nightclubs. Now I am studying social media and mobile technologies sensitive to changing contexts of use given GPS, mobile, and WiFi networks.

Projects

My latest projects during my time at Northwestern.

Volunteered Geographic Information

Millions of users generate and post content daily. What is the relationship between their offline context while posting (time, place) and their content in the media stream?

Location-based Real Time Apps

A growing number of apps connect nearby others for near-immediate in-person interaction, ranging from coffee to casual sex. How do users protray themselves to others in these spaces? What are in-person interactions like with someone you've just "met"?

Computer-Supported Group Critique

Novice civic designers face multiple challenges as they embark on tackling real world problems, including learning the design process and participating in critiques. How can a computer-supported group critique system fit into novice designer studios?

  • DIS 2014

Perspective Taking and Games

Students of politics regularly default to simple partisan arguments without understanding the range of political ideologies within policy debates. How may a game help teach perspective taking and political ideologies to better students' public policy skills?
